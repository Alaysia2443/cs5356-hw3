For my homework, I experimented with a CSS property, the box-shadow property, and the video element in HTML. I added a box shadow to my photo to make it stand out against the background. This property allowed my image to appear to float above the background. I learned that I can tweak the values in the property, including the horizontal and vertical offset, blur, and color, to create the desired shadow effect. I also included a video element on my site to feature a video from my trip to Dubai. The video tag has controls and can be styled using CSS. Using this element helped me understand one of the limitations of HTML. HTML, or at least the version in my Visual Studio code, does not accept MOV video types. This required me to find a solution by converting the video to MP4 and using type="video/mp4." Overall, it was interesting to see how easily I could integrate a video into my website and make an image in the background stand out. These two features added a new dimension to the layout and visuals of my design.
